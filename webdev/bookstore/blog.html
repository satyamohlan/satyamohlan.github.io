A NEW AGE OF TECHNOLOGY

Introducing Project VISION: India’s entry into the assistive tech market The World Health Organisation (WHO) estimates that there are more than 250 million people in the world today with partial or complete visual impairment. But, only a handful have access to braille learning, assistive technology and other resources, while every single one of them has to live with a strong dependence on others for day-to-day activities like crossing a road or navigating their way through traffic. Hence, a new market has emerged for assistive technology, which provides creative solutions that enable individuals with disabilities to be more independent, productive, and included in society and community life. Project Vision aims to achieve just that. Through our state of the art technology, we intend on making a device that would provide spacial awareness to the blind population and the ability to navigate one’s own surroundings independently. With text-to-speech (TTS), facial recognition, haptic feedback system and many other features, we aim to help our users understand and perceive their surroundings in a whole new way. We hope to help society recognize these individuals as equals, and not as people who can be looked down upon. This not only violates their fundamental rights, but also neglects us from tapping into a vast economic opportunity, as a denied market. Here at Project Vision we are looking to go beyond the charity model, the kind that often leaves our customers feeling indebted; like a charity case. We wish to create a sustainable and profitable business, all while increasing accessibility of our products to the users. It's a new age of technology, equality and empowerment.
